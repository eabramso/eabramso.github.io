About me

I am currently a senior at Macalester College in Saint Paul, Minnesota, where I’m majoring in Geography with a focus on urban studies.

My interests include urban and regional planning, human-environment interaction, sustainability, and public policy. I’m particularly interested in using cartography and data analysis to investigate and communicate about issues of inequity and power imbalance.

I love cartography because it has the power to integrate art with functionality, and to communicate complex information in a digestible and eye-catching way. In my free time I enjoy making art by hand, as well as digitizing my art and reformulating it with Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator.

I am a relative newcomer to RStudio, but since taking an intro to Data Science class, I often find myself spending my free time exploring datasets with R and doing extensive googling to find new ways to visualize data and solidify my skill set.

Aside from that, you can often find me listening to podcasts (a couple of current favorites are Undiscovered and The Daily), trying out new crockpot recipes, and when the weather’s warmer, exploring the Twin Cities by bike and by foot.
